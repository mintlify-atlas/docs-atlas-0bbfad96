## Welcome to University Life!

You're here - now it's time to make the most of your university experience. Chapter is your companion throughout your degree, helping you stay organized, connected, and engaged with everything campus life has to offer.

<Note>
  Chapter adapts to your year of study. First years see different features and recommendations compared to final year students.
</Note>

## First Year Priorities

If you're in first year, Chapter helps you settle in and build your university experience:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Import Your Timetable">
    Sync your lecture and seminar schedule to Chapter's calendar. Never miss a class and see what's happening each day.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Join Societies">
    Freshers Fair can be overwhelming. Use Chapter to browse all societies, see what events they're running, and join ones that interest you.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Explore Campus">
    Learn where everything is - lecture halls, libraries, cafes, study spaces, and social areas using the interactive campus map.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Attend Freshers Events">
    Don't miss out! Chapter shows all freshers week events with times, locations, and which friends are going.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Find Your People">
    Connect with coursemates, flatmates, and others who share your interests. University is about people as much as academics.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pro Tips for Student Life

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Balance is key">
    - Attend lectures and keep on top of work
    - But also make time for socializing and societies
    - Don't sacrifice sleep for either
    - First year often "doesn't count" - use it to experiment and find what works
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Get involved early">
    - Join societies in first term while everyone's welcoming newcomers
    - It's harder to break into established groups later
    - You can always leave if something isn't for you
    - Committee positions look great on CVs
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use university resources">
    - You're paying for student services - use them!
    - Free gym, counseling, careers advice, academic support
    - Most universities have amazing facilities
    - Don't wait until you're struggling to ask for help
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Make the most of student life">
    - Student discounts on everything (UNiDAYS, Student Beans)
    - Take advantage of cheap/free events
    - Travel while you can (student cards for trains/coaches)
    - This freedom and flexibility won't last forever
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Document the journey">
    - Take photos and videos
    - You'll want these memories
    - Use Journey Timeline to organize them
    - Three years goes faster than you think
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Struggling? You're Not Alone

University isn't always easy. If you're finding things difficult:

<Warning>
  Mental health struggles are common at university. Reaching out for support is a sign of strength, not weakness.
</Warning>

**Available support:**
- **University counseling** - Free, confidential, often same-week appointments
- **Student union advice** - For any issue, academic or personal
- **Course mates and friends** - Chances are others feel the same
- **Personal tutor** - They're there to support you
- **Samaritans** - 116 123, free 24/7 for anyone who needs to talk
